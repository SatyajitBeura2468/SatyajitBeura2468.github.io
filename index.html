The Making of a Mind

Hi, I'm Satyajit. I am a Class 11 student in the Arjuna NEET batch, but my interests go far beyond the standard curriculum. I exist at the intersection of Advanced Physics and Computer Science.

My goal is to merge theoretical science with visual technology. Whether it's coding simulations in Three.js or developing new scientific theories, I believe in "Trying To Do Better" every single day.

Research & Projects

1. Dimensional Memory Encoding (DME)

I am developing a theoretical framework called DME. It explores the possibility that human memory and consciousness might rely on encoding data across dimensional boundaries, utilizing principles of quantum mechanics. I am currently refining a paper on this topic.

2. Pollution Eating Paint

A project focused on environmental engineering. I worked on designing a chemical compound that can be added to wall paint to absorb and neutralize harmful air pollutants. This project was developed for the National Science Exhibition.

3. AI & Visual Simulations

I use code to visualize the invisible. My recent work includes interactive simulations of fluid dynamics (Bernoulli's Principle) and creating surreal digital art that blends sci-fi themes with scientific accuracy.
